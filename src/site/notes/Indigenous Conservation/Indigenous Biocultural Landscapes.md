# Tukha people case study
- Conflicts with Mongolian national park
- Specific regulations on hunting, harvesting of plants which are inflexible and not compatible with history
	- Loss of knowledge - since hunting is not allowed fewer young men come to camps in the summer
	- Food insecurity
	- Loss of crafting techniques from hunting
	- Forbidden to hunt wolves and bears because of tradition - they move away instead
		- It's believed that the only one who can kill a wolf is one who has a spiritual power
		- Often they will hire a hunter from far away to come kill the wolf since they believe the wolf will take revenge.
		- Compensation for loss of livestock by snow leopard
- Girls hair is cut in May with reindeer birth - boy's hair cut in September for hunting
- Many specific words related to reindeer herding in the language
	- Words being lost because of park restrictions
